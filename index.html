Will A Tactical Vote Work?

If Labour, Lib Dem and Green supporters vote tactically, could they beat the Tories?

The following simulator re-allocates Labour, Lib Dem and Green votes based on tactical voting.
It uses the tactical voting recommendations from the site www.tactical2017.com
This CRUDE projection is based on the 2015 General Election vote counts combined with recent poll data to provide a basic uniform swing.
You can set the parameters such as the amount of people participating in the tactical vote and the factor polls have on determining swing.

Explanation of logic, limitations and false assumptions

  • The tactical recommendations can be for parties other than Labour, Lib Dem and Green (e.g. SNP, Plaid Cymru) and therefore such parties will receive tactical votes in this simulation. However, for simplicity, this simulation assumes that supporters of parties outside the aforementioned 3, will not participate in the tactical vote themselves, hence their initial votes are not re-allocated.

  • The percentage of tactical vote participation does not take into account the people who already tactically voted in the last election. (Unfortunately there's no way of knowing how many of the last election's Labour/Lib Dem votes were Green Supporters tactically voting for example.

  • Polls have been known to be very wrong in recent times
    On the other hand, a lot has happened since 2015 so the 2015 results aren't much to go on either.

  • The national level polls don't show how factors such as Brexit and Indyref2 will affect some constuencies' votes more than others and this could have a major effect on the tactical vote.

  • Some of the recommendations from www.tactical2017.com do not give a clear singular choice of party, in these cases I simply choose one for simplicity, defaulting to Labour in most cases - just - because. You can see the mapping I use here

  • The simulation falsely assumes that the parties have candidates standing for all the same constituencies as they in the last General Election, which is not the case and may have an impact on the tactical vote result. As an improvement I would have to integrate data around current candidates.
    Perhaps we can assume if a party has withdrawn from a constituency then it didn't receive much votes there in 2015 in which case impact is minimal.

  • In some cases the number of Labour seats goes down due to the tactical vote. This is due to the predictions from the people at www.tactical2017.com based on their current political knowledge, differing from my Uniform National Swing projections. Namely, they predict that one or more currently held Labour seats will get less votes than the Lib Dems this time around.
    As a further improvement I could change my UNS projection to include exceptions matching the predictions of www.tactical2017.com but this would be too much work.
